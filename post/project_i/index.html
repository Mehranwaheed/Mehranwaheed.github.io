Project I: Sunspot and sunflares

January 25, 2024

Project Description:

In this project, I delved into the fascinating realm of solar flare prediction, leveraging data on sunspots and solar flares. The goal was to develop a robust model capable of forecasting severe solar flares, with a particular focus on X-class flares.

Key Steps and Achievements:

Data Exploration and Cleaning:

Conducted thorough exploratory data analysis (EDA) to understand patterns in sunspot data. Merged sunspot and solar flare datasets, enhancing the richness of information.

Imbalanced Data Challenge:

Identified imbalances in flare classifications, especially for rare X-class flares. Addressed data imbalances through techniques like retraining with balanced datasets.

Metric Shift to F1 Score:

Transitioned to the F1 score as an evaluation metric to balance precision and recall. Emphasized better precision to enhance predictions for X-class flares.

Precision-Recall Tradeoff:

Explored the precision-recall tradeoff to fine-tune models and achieve better results. Applied this tradeoff effectively, especially with Support Vector Machines (SVM). Conclusion and Implications: The project’s journey involved overcoming challenges associated with imbalanced data and optimizing models for the intricate task of solar flare prediction. Insights gained contribute to the ongoing efforts in space weather forecasting, potentially impacting various technological systems affected by solar activity.

This project serves as a testament to my skills in data analysis, machine learning, and problem-solving, showcasing my ability to navigate complex challenges and derive meaningful insights from diverse datasets. The pursuit of accuracy in predicting solar flares aligns with my commitment to advancing knowledge in domains with real-world implications.
